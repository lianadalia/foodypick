# User Personas — foodypick Helsinki

**Project:** foodypick
**Date:** 2026-03-03
**Context:** Helsinki metropolitan area (Helsinki, Espoo, Vantaa)
**Research basis:** Finnish food delivery market data, Statistics Finland lifestyle surveys, Wolt/K-Ruoka user reviews

---

## Persona 1 — Mikael

**"If it's not tracked to the minute, I'm already annoyed."**

### Profile
| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Age** | 31 |
| **Occupation** | Backend developer at a startup in Kallio |
| **Location** | Kallio, Helsinki |
| **Living situation** | Studio apartment, lives alone |
| **Income** | €62,000/year |
| **Languages** | Finnish, English |
| **Devices** | iPhone 15, MacBook Pro |

### Personality
Methodical, efficiency-obsessed, low tolerance for friction. Considers cooking a waste of billable time on weekdays. Orders 4–6 times a week, almost exclusively dinner. Treats food delivery as infrastructure, not a treat.

### Goals
- Get dinner with zero decision fatigue after a long coding session
- Pay a predictable, honest total — hates discovering a 2 € "small order fee" at payment
- Track exactly where his food is in real time
- Reorder the same two or three meals he already knows he likes

### Frustrations
- Delivery time estimates that shift by 20 minutes without explanation
- Apps that bury fees until the checkout screen
- Too many filter options — he wants a "just show me what arrives in under 30 min" toggle
- Mandatory upsell modals ("Add a drink?") between every step

### App behaviour
- Opens the app around 18:30–19:00 Mon–Thu
- Reorders from history 70% of the time, rarely browses new restaurants
- Immediately checks ETA before adding anything to cart
- Sets delivery note once ("Door code 1453") and expects it to persist

### Quote
> "I have three restaurants I trust. I don't need discovery, I need the thing I want in front of me immediately."

## Persona 2 — Aino

**"I want something good, not just something fast."**

### Profile
| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Age** | 26 |
| **Occupation** | UX designer at a design agency in Kamppi |
| **Location** | Töölö, Helsinki |
| **Living situation** | Shares a flat with one flatmate |
| **Income** | €38,000/year |
| **Languages** | Finnish, Swedish, English |
| **Devices** | Samsung Galaxy S24, iPad |

### Personality
Visually driven, socially conscious, adventurous eater. Foodypick would be her third delivery app — she already uses Wolt and occasionally K-Ruoka. She notices bad UI before she notices bad food. Orders 2–3 times a week, often on Friday evening or Sunday lunch.

### Goals
- Find restaurants she hasn't tried before, especially independent spots
- See dietary information (vegan options, allergens) clearly before she commits
- Order for two — portions should be described accurately
- Feel good about the order: sustainable packaging badges matter to her

### Frustrations
- Apps that show beautiful food photography but deliver something completely different
- Vague allergen info ("may contain") without specifics
- Cuisine filters that lump "Asian" as a single category
- Delivery fees that make a €12 bowl cost €19 total

### App behaviour
- Browses on Saturday morning to plan what to order that evening
- Reads item descriptions carefully; dietary badges are a hard filter
- Likely to screenshot a menu item to discuss with her flatmate before ordering
- Leaves detailed reviews ("the tofu was crispy but the sauce was too sweet")

### Quote
> "I'm not in a rush. I want the app to help me find something I'll actually be excited about, not just the nearest burger."

## Persona 3 — Tommi

**"My kids will only eat three things. I just need it to arrive before they melt down."**

### Profile
| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Age** | 41 |
| **Occupation** | Project manager at a construction firm |
| **Location** | Espoo (Tapiola) |
| **Living situation** | House, married with two children (7 and 10) |
| **Income** | €75,000/year (household) |
| **Languages** | Finnish |
| **Devices** | iPhone 13 (work) |

### Personality
Pragmatic, time-pressured, risk-averse. Orders delivery as a solution to a chaotic evening, not a treat. Highly brand-loyal once a restaurant passes the "kids will eat it" test. Willing to pay more for reliability. Orders 1–2 times per week, mostly Friday or when both parents are working late.

### Goals
- Find family-friendly restaurants with kid-portion options
- Large, readable menus without having to zoom in
- Pay once — doesn't want to re-enter card details every order
- On-time delivery: 19:00 dinner is serious when school nights are involved

### Frustrations
- Restaurants with no photo of the actual dish — can't guess if his kids will accept it
- Checkout forms that time out and lose cart contents
- Apps that require creating an account before seeing local restaurants
- Min. order values that force unnecessary extras when he just wants two pizzas

### App behaviour
- Orders from the same 3–4 restaurants repeatedly (pizza, hamburgers, Asian noodles)
- Uses the address autocomplete to save the family home address
- Prefers cash-on-delivery fallback in case card issues arise
- Abandons checkout if the estimated arrival exceeds 45 minutes

### Quote
> "Fridays are the only night nobody has to cook. It has to work, every time."

## Persona 4 — Priya

**"I'm a student, so I'm always looking for the best deal."**

### Profile
| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Age** | 23 |
| **Occupation** | Master's student in computer science at Aalto University |
| **Location** | Otaniemi, Espoo (student housing) |
| **Living situation** | Student flat, shares kitchen with 3 others |
| **Income** | ~€900/month (grant + part-time tutoring) |
| **Languages** | English, Hindi, basic Finnish |
| **Devices** | Android (Pixel 7a), laptop |

### Personality
Budget-conscious, digitally native, socially connected. Food delivery is occasional — she usually cooks — but orders when deadlines pile up or when celebrating with friends. Very comparison-savvy: opens three apps before deciding. Promotions and free-delivery thresholds drive her decisions.

### Goals
- Find cheap but filling options close to campus or on the tram route home
- Use discount codes and student deals whenever possible
- Split an order with flatmates (large shareable dishes)
- Discover new cuisines without committing to a full price order (small portions)

### Frustrations
- €3–4 delivery fees that represent 25% of the meal cost
- Promo codes that expire or don't apply to the restaurants she wants
- No vegetarian filter that actually works (still shows meat-only menus)
- Slow apps — she's ordering between lecture slides, not at leisure

### App behaviour
- Checks the app late at night (22:00–23:30) when studying
- Heavily uses filters: price low-to-high, "under 30 min", vegetarian
- Applies promo codes even if they save only €1
- Shares order with flatmates frequently; total split across 2–4 people

### Quote
> "If the delivery fee is more than two euros I'm probably cooking ramen. But if there's a deal I'll tell everyone in the group chat."

## Persona 5 — Helena

**"I just want something warm. I'm not interested in learning a new app."**

### Profile
| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Age** | 58 |
| **Occupation** | Senior nurse, Helsinki University Hospital |
| **Location** | Lauttasaari, Helsinki |
| **Living situation** | Owns a flat, recently empty-nester |
| **Income** | €52,000/year |
| **Languages** | Finnish, Swedish |
| **Devices** | iPhone SE (2022), desktop PC at home |

### Personality
Comfortable with technology but not enthusiastic about it. Uses her phone for calls, WhatsApp, and banking — delivery apps are a newer habit adopted after her children moved out. Orders once a week, usually after a long hospital shift. Values simplicity, warmth in brand tone, and a phone number she can call if something goes wrong.

### Goals
- Get a hot, complete meal delivered without making decisions
- Understand the total cost before she commits — no surprises
- Trust the restaurant: hygiene ratings and user reviews matter
- Have a saved address and payment method that "just work" every time

### Frustrations
- Small text, low-contrast UI — hard to read after a 12-hour shift
- Too many steps: she has pressed "back" by accident and lost a full cart
- No clear way to contact the restaurant or rider if something is missing
- Animated modals and popups that feel chaotic and undermine her trust

### App behaviour
- Orders around 20:00–21:00 after shifts, 1–2 days per week
- Prefers familiar Finnish comfort food: soup, fish, traditional lunch plates
- Reads reviews before trying any new restaurant (minimum 4.0 stars)
- Would call customer support if delivery was wrong; expects a phone number

### Quote
> "I just want it to be easy. I'm tired. I don't want to feel stupid using an app."
