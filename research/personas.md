# User Personas — foodypick

**Project:** foodypick
**Date:** 2026-03-03
**Research basis:** Competitive analysis + analogous domain interviews + food delivery app store reviews

---

## Persona 1 — Marcus

**"I need it fast and I need to know exactly what I'm paying."**

### Profile
| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Age** | 28 |
| **Occupation** | Software engineer at a mid-size fintech |
| **Location** | Central Manchester, UK |
| **Living situation** | Renting a flat alone |
| **Income** | £48,000/year |
| **Orders per week** | 3–4 |
| **Devices** | iPhone 15 Pro (primary), iPad at home |
| **Payment** | Apple Pay, occasionally card |

### Photo Description
Marcus: young Black man, casual professional attire, typically orders on his phone during or just after a work-from-home session. Often seen with headphones around his neck.

### Goals
- **Primary:** Get a satisfying meal delivered within 30 minutes without having to think too hard about it.
- **Secondary:** Discover new cuisines occasionally, but mostly reorders from trusted restaurants.
- **Tertiary:** Keep monthly food spend visible and under control — uses his bank app to track it.

### Frustrations
- **Hidden fees:** "I've clicked through to checkout only to find the price is 25% higher than I expected. I just close the app."
- **Forced sign-up:** "I downloaded a competitor's app last month and they made me create an account before I could even see what restaurants were near me. Deleted it immediately."
- **ETA lies:** "If you say 20 minutes and it's 45, I'd rather you just said 40–50 minutes from the start."
- **Endless upsells:** "I don't want a subscription. I want to order food. Stop asking."
- **Cart clearing:** "If I accidentally go back and my cart disappears, I'm done."

### Tech Comfort
High. Early adopter. Has 6 food delivery apps installed and switches based on offer codes. Familiar with Face ID payments, Live Activities, widget stacks.

### Quote
> "My time is worth money. The best food app is the one that gets out of its own way — no surprises, no friction, food at the door."

### Relevance to foodypick
Marcus is the **power user / frequent returner**. He will heavily use the "Order Again" shortcut, Apple Pay at checkout, and fee breakdown transparency. His high switch rate means foodypick must nail reliability and trust from the first order.

## Persona 2 — Priya

**"I want to try something different but I can't afford to make a mistake."**

### Profile
| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Age** | 21 |
| **Occupation** | 2nd-year Computer Science student |
| **Location** | Sheffield, UK (shared student house) |
| **Living situation** | 4 housemates, shared kitchen |
| **Income** | ~£800/month (student loan + part-time job) |
| **Orders per week** | 1–2 (often shared with housemates) |
| **Devices** | Samsung Galaxy S23 (primary), laptop |
| **Payment** | Debit card, sometimes splits via Monzo with friends |

### Photo Description
Priya: South Asian woman in her early 20s, usually on her phone while studying. Orders later in the evening (9–11 PM) after a study session. Often orders with 1–2 housemates — they gather around the phone to choose.

### Goals
- **Primary:** Find affordable, filling food that doesn't break her weekly budget.
- **Secondary:** Discover interesting new cuisines she hasn't tried before.
- **Tertiary:** Share the order with housemates and split cost easily.

### Frustrations
- **Budget anxiety:** "I get to checkout and the delivery fee plus service fee means a £14 burger actually costs me £20. That's my lunch for tomorrow too."
- **Photo quality:** "If there's no picture of the food I'm not ordering it. I don't want a gamble."
- **Allergen information:** Priya is lacto-vegetarian. "I spend ages reading descriptions trying to figure out if something has meat or fish sauce in it. Just tell me."
- **Late-night closures:** "My favourite places close at 10 PM. I need to know if they're open before I spend 10 minutes building a cart."
- **Promo code failures:** "I see a 25% off code on Instagram and then it doesn't apply at checkout because I haven't ordered before from this specific restaurant on this specific day."

### Tech Comfort
Medium-high. Uses apps natively and fluently but doesn't seek out feature depth. Expects things to work intuitively without reading instructions.

### Quote
> "I don't mind paying for good food, but I need to know the real price upfront. And if it has nuts, I need to know before I order, not when I open the container."

### Relevance to foodypick
Priya represents the **cost-sensitive discoverer**. She validates the dietary badge feature (V, VE, GF, N chips on every item), the fee transparency on restaurant cards, and the need for high-quality item photos. Her shared ordering behaviour is a future co-order feature opportunity.

## Persona 3 — Sandra

**"I just need something reliable that the whole family will eat."**

### Profile
| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Age** | 41 |
| **Occupation** | Part-time accountant + primary carer |
| **Location** | Suburban Bristol, UK |
| **Living situation** | Semi-detached house with husband and 2 children (ages 8 and 11) |
| **Income** | Household ~£62,000/year |
| **Orders per week** | 0–1 (weekend treat, occasionally mid-week exhaustion) |
| **Devices** | iPhone 14 (primary), iPad shared with kids |
| **Payment** | Saved card, occasionally contactless terminal at door |

### Photo Description
Sandra: white British woman, late 30s/early 40s, usually orders on a Friday evening after the kids' activities. High-stress decision-maker — is managing kids' preferences, her husband's preferences, and her own simultaneously.

### Goals
- **Primary:** Get a family meal sorted quickly on a tired evening without drama.
- **Secondary:** Find restaurants that have something for picky children AND adults.
- **Tertiary:** Avoid making a mistake — a wrong order with two unhappy children is a bad evening.

### Frustrations
- **Menu complexity:** "I'm ordering for 4 people with different tastes. I need a clear menu, not a scroll of 200 items with no categories."
- **Allergy stress:** Her son has a tree nut allergy. "I spend ages checking every item. I shouldn't have to hunt for allergen information."
- **ETA anxiety:** "If the app says 30 minutes and it's been 45 and no update, I'm on edge. My kids are hungry and asking every 5 minutes."
- **Minimum order thresholds:** "I click on a restaurant and then find out there's a £20 minimum order for our postcode. Why isn't this shown on the restaurant card?"
- **Payment faff:** "I don't want to type in a 16-digit card number on my phone while the kids are fighting. Saved payment should just work."

### Tech Comfort
Medium. Comfortable with apps she uses regularly but doesn't explore unfamiliar features. Appreciates clear navigation and prefers simplicity over density.

### Quote
> "If the food is good and it arrives when they say it will, I'll order again. If it's wrong or late with no communication, I'll delete the app."

### Relevance to foodypick
Sandra is the **reliability-focused parent buyer**. She validates the allergen tag system (especially the nut "N" badge), upfront min-order and fee display on restaurant cards, and proactive tracking updates. Her payment friction validates Apple/Google Pay shortcut at checkout.
